## Resume Builder App Plan (Text-Only Master Plan)

### Goal

Build a resume builder that reliably converts job seekers into paying users by putting the paywall on **exports + premium templates**, using **PayPal** as the main checkout (PayPal balance + cards).

---

## 1) What You’re Building

A web app that lets users:

* pick a template
* fill in resume sections with a guided step-by-step form
* see a live preview
* export as PDF (and DOCX for paid)
* optionally share a public resume link

---

## 2) Core Audience (Who Pays Fast)

People actively applying right now:

* recent graduates
* career switchers
* people who keep getting rejected
* people who need “one clean resume” quickly

These users pay because they’re in urgency mode.

---

## 3) Business Model (Designed for $500/week)

You need income that’s not random. This is the structure:

### Plans

**Free**

* 1 resume
* basic templates only
* PDF export has a watermark
* no DOCX export

**One-time**

* $14.99 “Export Pack”
* removes watermark
* allows PDF + DOCX export for that resume (or limited exports)

**Pro Subscription**

* $9.99/month
* unlimited resumes
* premium templates
* no watermark
* PDF + DOCX export
* cover letter builder (Pro)
* duplicate resume for each job posting (Pro)

### Why this converts

* Free users still get value
* They only hit payment when they need the thing they truly want: **export**
* One-time option catches people who hate subscriptions
* Subscription stacks and becomes predictable over time

---

## 4) PayPal Payment Approach (Main Checkout)

PayPal is the only payment platform.

PayPal supports:

* PayPal balance
* debit/credit cards (guest checkout)
* subscriptions + one-time payments

### Payment Rule

The app never “trusts” a payment screenshot or the frontend.
Only these count:

* PayPal payment capture confirmation (one-time)
* PayPal subscription active confirmation (recurring)

---

## 5) User Journey (The Funnel)

This is the exact flow that makes money:

1. User lands on your site
2. They click “Start Resume”
3. They pick a template (free templates visible)
4. They fill out resume using a simple wizard
5. They see their resume preview looking professional
6. They press “Export”
7. App says:

   * Free: watermark PDF available
   * Want clean export + DOCX? Pay with PayPal
8. They pay
9. They export instantly
10. They save account + come back later

This is the reliable conversion moment.

---

## 6) Pages You Need (Minimum Set)

### Public

* Landing page (value + examples + “Start” button)
* Templates page (gallery previews)
* Pricing page (free vs one-time vs pro)
* Login/Signup

### App (Logged in)

* Dashboard (list resumes + create new)
* Builder (wizard + preview)
* Export screen (paywall + download links)
* Settings (plan info + billing)

Optional:

* Public share page if user enables it

---

## 7) Resume Builder Sections (MVP)

Keep it lean but complete:

* Contact (name, email, phone, city/state, LinkedIn, portfolio)
* Professional Summary (2–4 lines)
* Experience (job title, company, location, dates, bullet points)
* Education (degree, school, dates)
* Skills (tags)
* Projects (optional but good)

This covers 95% of users.

---

## 8) Templates Plan (Simple + Scalable)

Templates are “skins,” not separate resume formats.

### MVP templates

* Modern (clean, popular)
* Classic ATS (simple, safe)
* Minimal (one-column)

### Premium templates (Pro)

* Executive
* Creative
* Tech-focused
* Two-column (optional, some ATS issues—label as “Designer template”)

Each template uses the same resume data but renders differently.

---

## 9) Export Plan (What Users Pay For)

**Export is your main money button.**

* Free export: PDF with watermark (“Made with ResumeForge”)
* Paid export:

  * PDF no watermark
  * DOCX export
  * optional: matching cover letter export

Export should feel instant:

* “Generate”
* “Download PDF”
* “Download DOCX”

---

## 10) ATS Value (Light but Real)

You don’t need a complex AI to provide ATS help.

MVP ATS checks:

* Missing contact info
* No summary (suggest adding one)
* Too few bullet points in experience
* Bad date formatting
* Overly long paragraphs
* Uncommon headings (recommend standard ones)

Pro ATS upgrade:

* Paste job description → highlight missing keywords

---

## 11) Data You Store (Conceptually)

You store:

* user account
* resume JSON data (all fields)
* selected template
* export history
* payment status (free / one-time / pro)

Key concept:
**One resume = one saved JSON object**
Templates just “display” it.

---

## 12) Feature Boundaries (So It Launches Fast)

### Must-have for MVP launch:

* login/signup
* create resume
* edit resume in wizard
* live preview
* template selection (3)
* PDF export (watermark)
* PayPal one-time purchase to remove watermark
* PayPal subscription to unlock Pro
* download paid export

### Not needed before launch:

* AI writing
* job board integrations
* interview tracker
* fancy animations

Launch first. Upgrade later.

### Funnel:

TikTok → landing page → builder → export paywall → PayPal → money

You combine 2 streams:

1. **One-time Export Pack buyers**
2. **Monthly Pro subscribers**

Example weekly targets:

* 20 export packs/week × $14.99 ≈ $300/week
* 20 Pro subs/week × $9.99 ≈ $200/week recurring momentum
  That’s your $500/week shape, and it grows because subs stay.
